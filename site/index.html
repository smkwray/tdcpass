Unexpected TDC raises match total deposits on impact — but less than one-for-one

When unexpected transaction deposit creation (TDC) rises, total bank deposits rise — but the non-TDC component falls. The impact-stage result is the clearest finding. Medium-horizon persistence remains unsettled. This is a methods and reproducibility public stopping point, not a finished mechanism paper.

What is TDC? TDC is the Treasury-attributed component of deposits: an upstream quarterly estimate of how much deposit growth is tied to Treasury-related flows. It is not the TGA or literal Treasury deposit balances. Source: tdcest.
More detail

In this repo, TDC is imported from the upstream tdcest project. The specific headline estimate used here is the bank-only transaction-flow series tdc_base_bank_only_ru_flow, which appears in this repo as tdc_bank_only_qoq. Conceptually, it measures reserve-user net acquisition of marketable Treasuries, minus Treasury operating-cash drain, plus positive Fed remittances only.

Exact-Baseline Impulse Responses

Primary Result

Cumulative responses to a one-standard-deviation unexpected TDC shock, estimated via identity-preserving local projections on 68 usable quarters (2009Q1–2025Q4). Shaded bands are bootstrap 95% confidence intervals.

Less than one-for-one. TDC rises sharply on impact (+$128B) but total deposits gain only +$56B because the non-TDC residual drops −$73B. The gap between the TDC line and the total-deposit line is the crowd-out signal — each dollar of unexpected TDC does not create a full dollar of new deposits. On impact, a $100B increase in TDC is associated with roughly a $40–45B increase in total deposits.

Period Sensitivity

Secondary

How stable is the impact-stage signal across sub-periods? The chart and table compare the headline all-usable sample against three sub-windows. Impact (h0) is more stable than medium-horizon persistence (h4). The COVID/post-COVID window shows the strongest impact-stage total-deposit response.

Impact (h0) by period

Persistence varies. All four periods show a negative non-TDC residual on impact, but the h4 patterns diverge: post-GFC early and pre-COVID windows lose separation, while COVID/post-COVID retains a positive total-deposit signal at h4. The COVID/post-COVID period has the largest impact-stage total-deposit point estimate (+$91B). Medium-horizon persistence remains unsettled.

Robustness & Limits

Diagnostics

The treatment shock passes all six quality gates. Robustness is intentionally narrow: exact-baseline treatment, control-set, sample, and period sensitivity. The exact identity baseline is primary; the approximate dynamic LP path is a secondary robustness check. Ratio-based pass-through and crowd-out headlines are out of scope.

Diagnostic Value Status
What remains unsettled. Medium-horizon persistence is sensitive to period and sample. The approximate dynamic LP path diverges from the exact baseline at key horizons. Structural proxy cross-checks are mixed. Headline pass-through ratios are out of scope until a dimensionally coherent first-stage gate is resolved. Persistence and mechanism remain unsettled.

Data & Methods

Reproducibility

All results derive from public quarterly data (FRED, Call Reports). The exact identity-preserving baseline uses a frozen rolling 40-quarter ridge unexpected-TDC shock with nested circular block bootstrap inference (4q blocks, 250 reps).
